### SHARING AND FEEDBACK

#### Overall Evaluation

**1. Working Environment**  
The internship environment at AWS Vietnam brings an extremely friendly, open, yet highly professional experience. I was provided with maximum conditions to access rich resources and modern technical tools necessary for the Cloud/DevOps hands-on roadmap. Especially, the direct face-to-face meetings of the FCAJ group and community events organized at the Bitexco office not only expanded my horizon but also gave me a real sense of belonging to a cohesive technology collective. I hope that in the future, the program can alternate small-scale internal "Tech Talks" so that teams have opportunities to present products and learn from each other.

**2. Support from Mentor / Team Admin**  
I highly appreciate the education method of the Mentors: instead of just giving ready-made answers, the mentors laid out a very clear Cloud Journey roadmap and always encouraged me to read documents, experiment, and analyze error logs first. This guidance helped me become more confident when handling complex incidents related to networking, IAM authorization, or EKS deployment flows. The FCAJ admin team also operated extremely professionally, always supporting and resolving procedural queries quickly and constantly reminding us of report deadlines, helping me maintain a steady work pace throughout the 12 weeks.

**3. Relevance of Work to Academic Major**  
The internship program completely aligns with my career development direction in Cloud Computing & DevOps. Foundational knowledge from university lectures (such as object-oriented programming thinking with C#/Java, computer networks, and operating systems) became a solid launching pad helping me absorb new knowledge faster. Consequently, getting familiar with complex source control technologies (such as branch management via Git/SourceTree), infrastructure as code concepts (Terraform), and container management (Kubernetes) became highly practical, helping me bridge the gap between academic theory and actual corporate requirements.

**4. Learning & Skill Development Opportunities**  
The FCAJ training roadmap is indeed a goldmine of knowledge. Not only limited to technical operations, I was also trained in self-research skills through translating bilingual technical documents on the AWS blog. Constructing a complete CI/CD pipeline on my own (from pushing code to the repo to automated packaging and deployment via GitHub Actions) provided me with invaluable experience. Furthermore, participating in the FCAJ Community Day and FCAJ GameDay provided me with multi-dimensional perspectives on Artificial Intelligence (AI), comprehensive solution architecture, and paths to international certifications.

**5. Company Culture & Team Spirit**  
The culture of the FCAJ community is "sharing to grow together". Everyone is always open, not hesitant to ask questions, and extremely respectful of each individual's learning pace. During the most stressful practice weeks with a huge volume of knowledge about Microservices and Data Lakes, the mutually supportive atmosphere on communication channels helped me relieve pressure. There was no toxic competition, instead, a spirit of "pair-programming" and troubleshooting line-by-line together. This makes me proud to be part of the community.

**6. Internship Policies / Benefits**  
The program possesses an extremely clear weekly progress and objective evaluation framework. However, to make the experience for future cohorts more complete, I suggest that the organizers should have a deep-dive session in the very first week on Cloud Cost Management (FinOps). Providing resource cleanup checklists (Terminate/Delete) early will help interns build good habits and avoid unexpected costs from forgotten resources such as Load Balancers, NAT Gateways, or EKS Clusters.

#### Additional Questions

* **What did you find most satisfying during your internship?**  
  The most satisfying moment was when I saw the seamlessness of the system designed by my own hands: from a simple push code action to a branch, the continuous integration system automatically triggered, successfully built the Docker image, and smoothly deployed it onto the EKS cluster. Besides, fully completing the 12-week worklog with a lot of personal effort, along with the memories of networking at the Bitexco office, is truly a proud milestone in my student life.

* **What should the program improve for future interns?**  
  The program should consider adding weekly fixed "Office Hours" to solve difficult topics such as Networking (VPC) and Route Security in depth. Also, providing a standardized, concise status report template (Stand-up report) will help interns practice professional status reporting skills. The workload in the last few weeks (especially the Data & K8s parts) was quite heavy; distributing it more evenly or having guidelines prioritizing practice order would help manage time more effectively.

* **Would you recommend this internship to a friend? Why?**  
  Definitely yes. FCAJ is the perfect environment for any IT student who wants to commit seriously to the Cloud/DevOps path. However, I will also warn my friends that: this is not a place where someone holds your hand. Success or failure depends entirely on proactive self-study, ability to read technical documents, and persistence when facing bright red error screens.

#### Suggestions & Expectations

* **Suggestions to improve the internship experience:**  
  * Provide a short weekly progress report template following Agile standards (What was done — Where are the blockers — What is the next step).
  * Organize 1 to 2 "Demo Days" for intern groups to present their products (demonstrate pipeline or architecture), thereby practicing technology presentation skills.
  * Provide standard documentation or configuration templates for setting up cost alerts (AWS Billing Alerts) before starting expensive labs.

* **Would you like to stay connected with the FCAJ community in the future?**  
  Yes. I really look forward to maintaining active membership (Alumni) in the FCAJ community. I plan to continue following the learning roadmap, participate in AWS technology events, and stand ready to share my hands-on experiences to support new generations of interns.

* **Other comments:**  
  I would like to send my most sincere thanks to Mentors, the FCAJ admin team, and all my colleagues. The past period has successfully helped me transform from mere theoretical thinking to practical cloud operation thinking. This program is the perfect stepping stone, creating great motivation for me to confidently enter the exam for AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner and further Solutions Architect in the near future.
